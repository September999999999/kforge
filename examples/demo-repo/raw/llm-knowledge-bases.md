## Notes

LLM-maintained knowledge repos keep original material under `raw/` and compile
human-readable articles under `wiki/`.

The useful shift is not only asking questions over documents. The stronger
workflow is to make every useful answer add structure back into the repo through
claims, reviews, indexes, and outputs.

The main trust risk is a polished wiki with weak provenance. Durable claims
should cite local source files, and broad edits should be staged as review
artifacts before they rewrite wiki pages.
